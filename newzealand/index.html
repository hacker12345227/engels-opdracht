📖 Travel Diary

Arriving in New Zealand felt like stepping into a fantasy movie. Everywhere I looked there were mountains, green hills and lakes.

I visited Queenstown first, where I saw some of the most beautiful landscapes I had ever seen. The air felt fresh and the nature looked untouched.

Later, I explored Hobbiton, the famous movie set from The Lord of the Rings. Walking through the small green hills felt magical.

One evening, I watched the sunset near the mountains while everything became silent. It was one of the most peaceful moments of my trip.

🏔️ Tourist Attractions

  • Hobbiton Movie Set – A famous filming location from “The Lord of the Rings” and “The Hobbit” films, where visitors can explore the charming Hobbit village in Matamata.
  • Milford Sound – A breathtaking fjord in Fiordland National Park, known for its dramatic cliffs, waterfalls, and stunning natural scenery.
  • Queenstown – A popular adventure city surrounded by mountains and lakes, famous for activities like bungee jumping, skiing, and hiking.
  • Mount Cook National Park – Home to New Zealand’s highest mountain, Aoraki / Mount Cook, offering glaciers, alpine views, and hiking trails.

📜 Historical Facts

  • The Māori were the first inhabitants – The Māori people settled in New Zealand around 1250–1300 AD and developed a rich cultural heritage and traditions.
  • British settlers arrived in the 1800s – European settlement began in the 19th century, significantly shaping the country’s modern history.
  • The Treaty of Waitangi was signed in 1840 – This important agreement between Māori chiefs and the British Crown is considered the founding document of New Zealand.
  • First to give women voting rights – New Zealand became the first self-governing country to grant women the right to vote in 1893.

🎌 Holidays

  • Waitangi Day – Observed on February 6, marking the signing of the Treaty of Waitangi and reflecting on New Zealand’s history and national identity.
  • Matariki Festival – A Māori New Year celebration based on the rising of the Pleiades star cluster, honoring reflection, renewal, and community.

🌿 Landscape

New Zealand is known for its dramatic and diverse natural scenery, including snow-capped mountains, lush forests, crystal-clear lakes, and deep fjords. Its landscapes are often considered some of the most beautiful in the world.

🎎 Culture

  • Strong Māori cultural influence – Māori traditions, language, and art are an important part of New Zealand’s national identity.
  • Love for outdoor activities – Hiking, camping, skiing, and water sports are popular due to the country’s natural environment.
  • Rugby is extremely popular – Rugby is considered the national sport, with the All Blacks being one of the world’s most famous teams.
  • Friendly and relaxed atmosphere – New Zealand is known for its welcoming people and laid-back lifestyle.
