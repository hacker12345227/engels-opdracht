📖 Travel Diary

Iceland felt completely different from every other country I visited. The cold air, giant waterfalls and dark volcanic landscapes looked unreal.

I visited Reykjavik first, where the city felt calm and peaceful. Even at night, everything felt quiet compared to other capitals.

Later, I explored glaciers and black sand beaches. Seeing ice and volcanoes in the same country was incredible.

The best moment was seeing the Northern Lights in the sky. The green lights moved slowly above the mountains and it felt magical.

❄️ Tourist Attractions

  • Blue Lagoon – A famous geothermal spa known for its milky-blue waters, rich in minerals, where visitors relax in naturally heated pools surrounded by lava fields.
  • Golden Circle – A popular sightseeing route that includes geysers, waterfalls, and Þingvellir National Park, showcasing Iceland’s dramatic geology.
  • Reykjavik – The capital city of Iceland, known for its colorful buildings, cultural landmarks, and vibrant arts and music scene.
  • Jökulsárlón Glacier Lagoon – A stunning glacial lake filled with floating icebergs that break off from a nearby glacier and drift toward the ocean.

📜 Historical Facts

  • Settled by Vikings in the 9th century – Norse explorers from Scandinavia were the first permanent settlers of Iceland.
  • One of the oldest parliaments in the world – The Althing, established in 930 AD, is considered one of the earliest parliamentary institutions still in existence.
  • Became independent in 1944 – Iceland gained full independence from Denmark during World War II and became a republic.
  • Strong connection to Norse mythology – Icelandic sagas preserve stories of Viking gods, heroes, and early settlers, forming a key part of cultural heritage.

🎌 Holidays

  • National Day of Iceland – Celebrated on June 17, marking the country’s independence and honoring its national identity with parades and festivities.
  • Þorrablót Festival – A traditional midwinter festival where Icelanders celebrate ancient Viking customs with traditional foods and gatherings.

🌋 Landscape

Iceland features some of the most dramatic natural landscapes in the world, including active volcanoes, massive glaciers, powerful waterfalls, black sand beaches, and vast lava fields shaped by constant volcanic activity.

🎎 Culture

  • Strong Viking heritage – Icelandic culture is deeply rooted in its Norse ancestry and preserved through sagas and storytelling traditions.
  • Love for nature and outdoor life – Hiking, glacier tours, and exploring geothermal areas are common parts of Icelandic lifestyle.
  • Geothermal energy is widely used – Iceland relies heavily on natural geothermal and hydroelectric energy for heating and electricity.
  • Traditional Icelandic folklore and legends – Stories of elves, trolls, and hidden people remain an important part of cultural imagination.
