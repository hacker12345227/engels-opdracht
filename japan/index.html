📖 Travel Diary

When I arrived in Japan, everything felt completely different from home. The lights, the speed of the city, and the silence of the temples all felt unreal.

In Tokyo, I walked through Shibuya Crossing. It was full of people from every direction. I felt small, but also excited to be part of it.

Later, I visited Kyoto. The atmosphere changed completely—quiet streets, old temples, and red gates everywhere. It felt peaceful and timeless.

The most beautiful moment was seeing Mount Fuji in the distance. It looked like a painting and I stood there for a long time just watching it.

🏯 Tourist Attractions

  • Mount Fuji – Japan’s tallest mountain and one of its most iconic landmarks. Many visitors hike it during summer or enjoy scenic views from nearby lakes and hot springs.
  • Fushimi Inari Shrine – A famous shrine in Kyoto known for its thousands of bright red torii gates that create beautiful walking paths through the forested hills.
  • Shibuya Crossing – Located in Tokyo, this world-famous intersection is known for its massive crowds, bright neon lights, and energetic city atmosphere.
  • Arashiyama Bamboo Forest – A peaceful bamboo grove in Kyoto where towering bamboo stalks line the pathways, creating a calm and scenic experience for visitors.

📜 Historical Facts

  • The Edo period brought peace
  • Japan modernized after 1868
  • Hiroshima & Nagasaki in 1945

🎌 Holidays

Japan has a mix of mountains, volcanoes, forests, and extremely modern cities.
