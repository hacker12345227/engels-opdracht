📖 Travel Diary

Peru felt mysterious from the moment I arrived. The mountains, old ruins and ancient stories made the country feel magical.

I visited Cusco first, a city full of history and culture. Walking through the streets felt like traveling back in time.

Later, I traveled to Machu Picchu high in the Andes Mountains. Seeing the ancient city hidden between the clouds was unforgettable.

One evening, I watched the sunset over the mountains while hearing traditional music nearby. It was one of the most special moments of my journey.

🏛️ Tourist Attractions

  • Machu Picchu – An ancient Inca citadel high in the Andes Mountains, known for its incredible stone architecture and breathtaking views. It is one of the New Seven Wonders of the World.
  • Cusco – A historic city that once served as the capital of the Inca Empire, featuring a blend of Inca ruins and Spanish colonial architecture.
  • Rainbow Mountain – A colorful mountain in the Andes famous for its natural stripes of red, yellow, and turquoise minerals created by geological processes.
  • Lake Titicaca – The highest navigable lake in the world, located on the border of Peru and Bolivia, known for its floating reed islands and Indigenous communities.

📜 Historical Facts

  • Home of the Inca Empire – Peru was the center of the powerful Inca civilization, which built advanced cities, roads, and agricultural systems across the Andes.
  • Spanish conquest began in the 1500s – The Inca Empire fell after Spanish arrival, leading to centuries of colonial rule.
  • Machu Picchu was rediscovered in 1911 – The site was brought to global attention by explorer Hiram Bingham, though it was never truly “lost” to local communities.
  • Ancient civilizations existed before the Incas – Cultures such as the Nazca and Moche thrived long before the rise of the Inca Empire.

🎌 Holidays

  • Inti Raymi Festival – An ancient Inca celebration honoring the Sun God Inti, now recreated as a major cultural festival in Cusco every June.
  • Peruvian Independence Day – Celebrated on July 28, marking Peru’s independence from Spanish rule with parades, fireworks, and national celebrations.

⛰️ Landscape

Peru has an incredibly diverse landscape that includes the Andes Mountains, the Amazon rainforest, coastal deserts, and fertile valleys. This variety makes it one of the most geographically rich countries in South America.

🎎 Culture

  • Strong Inca influence – Many traditions, ruins, and cultural practices are rooted in the legacy of the Inca civilization.
  • Traditional colorful clothing – In rural and Andean regions, vibrant textiles and traditional dress are still widely worn and culturally significant.
  • Famous traditional music and dancing – Folk music using instruments like pan flutes and drums plays a major role in celebrations and festivals.
  • Peruvian cuisine is world famous – Dishes like ceviche, lomo saltado, and causa reflect a blend of Indigenous, Spanish, and Asian influences.
